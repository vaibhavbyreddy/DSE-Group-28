\chapter{Functional Analysis}
The first step to determine the requirements for the aircraft is looking at its function in detail. This can be done using a functional flow diagram (FFD) (see Figure 4.2), which analyses a typical mission of the aircraft. From that, key functions can be identified and later formatted as a functional breakdown structure (FBS) (see Figure 4.3).

The functions follow typical mission of the aircraft, and additional scenarios such as missed approach. The mission is subdivided in the Launch, Flight Test and Recovery phases. The first covers everything from pre-flight preparations up to and including the take-off, the second - the main phase of flight when the high-speed tests are performed, the third - descend, approach attempts and landing. Non-main success scenarios involved are missed approach and flight termination by command or when contact with the aircraft is lost. The flight termination method is to be determined when a particular design concept is selected.
