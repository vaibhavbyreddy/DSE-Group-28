
\chapter{Sustainability Analysis}
Considerations regarding sustainability have become an important element of modern aerospace engineering. Therefore, in this project, it was very important to design for the sustainable construction and operation of the aircraft. Sustainability was taken into consideration at every step throughout the design process of HUGO. 

\section{Construction}

Historically, the construction of an aircraft has been a source of large emissions. The requirements for exotic materials with a high degree of processing lead to large energy usage and the generation of waste products. This has to be taken into consideration for the choice of material and design of the aircraft. Emphasis should be placed on reducing material usage by designing structurally efficient components. Another important consideration is that construction is a once-in-a-lifetime emission for the aircraft. This means that a weight saving measure which results in higher production emissions might be worth it for the long-term emission reduction due to weight saving.  

\section{Operation}
Since it was specified that the aircraft should use the Vulcan turbine engine, it will always generate emissions while in use. It was also specified that this turbine should be powered by SAF (sustainable aviation fuel) meaning it would have a non-petroleum source. This could theoretically lead to net-zero carbon emissions. Though the generation of SAF requires energy and this does not mitigate the emission of NOX typically attributed to turbine jets \cite{}. It is therefore also relevant to investigate modes of operation and implement systems that would decrease the amount of NOX.      


\section{End-Of-Life (EOL) Solutions}

The aircraft is designed to have a long lifetime (see HUG-STR-012) , but it is also important to design for the point when the structure needs to be scrapped. Recyclability should be prioritized for  the aircraft´s components. This is to be considered in the design of the aircraft. The material choice and bonding methods will influence how difficult (or whether it is even possible) to recycle the used material. 


\section{Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)}

To quantify the material input and output and the subsequent consequences of these the Life Cycle Assessment model is used to evaluate the more detailed concepts in later stages of the design process. Since a full Life Cycle Assessment is very time consuming and requires a complete finalised design it is relevant to use the streamlined Life Cycle Assessment. This is a version of the model which focuses on the differences between two designs and can be used to quickly evaluate more different designs\cite{Vogtlander2012}. 

\section{Environmental Benefits}

Another important aspect to consider is the overall environmental impact of the research enabled by the system. An emphasis was placed on how the system could enable a more detailed understanding of the operation of high-aspects ratio wings. 
%The implementation of such wings could lead to a potential reduction in fuel-usage of around 10\%   
The utilization of such technologies could potentially lead to major reductions in aviation-emissions (by some estimates up to 10\% \cite{Ma2024}). The major benefit of this product is the rapid testing and iteration of potential fuel-saving designs that it enables.
