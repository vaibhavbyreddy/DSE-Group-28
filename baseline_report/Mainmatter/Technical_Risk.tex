\section{Preliminary Specific Operation Risk Assessment (SORA)}
The SORA is a classification method used by JARUS to determine the risk posed by a UAV during operations \cite{} \todo{cite JARUS main}. Its main purpose is to determine whether the operations can be performed in a safe manner. The nominal SORA method is typically comprised of 10 steps encompassing the ground risk, air risk, mitigation performance requirements, assurance and integrity level estimation, and determination of containment requirements.

In this Section, a preliminary SORA classification will be performed for the UAV based on currently available data. It is expected that the final results of this methodology may change as as more information will be known about the UAV configuration and the mission area.

\subsection{Determination of the Air Risk Class (ARC) and Tactical Mitigation Performance Requirement (TMPR)}
The SORA methodology uses the operational airspace of the UAV to determine an intrinsic risk of collision with (manned) aircraft, which is expressed by the ARC \cite{}\todo{cite JARUS main}. For UAVs operating in atypical airspaces (such as reserved/restricted airspaces or defined drone testing areas), such as HUGO, an ARC-a score is automatically attributed.

The ARC can be lowered or modified via strategic and tactical mitigation means \cite{} \todo{cite JARUS main}. However, since ARC-a represents the lowest possible ARC score, such steps are not deemed as necessary for HUGO.

The TMPR refers to operational requirements which shall be applied to mitigate residual risks of air collisions with (manned) aircraft and achieve an acceptable level of safety during UAV operations \cite{}\todo{cite JARUS main}. For ARC-a operations, the risk of encounter of manned aircraft is deemed as "extremely low", and therefore no TMPR shall be applied (although it is possible for the CAA to require a demonstration of mitigation procedures for HUGO id deemed necessary)\cite{}\todo{cite JARUS Annex D}.

\subsection{Determination of Containment Requirements}
Size of adjacent area: 35km
Containment Requirements: Medium/High

Criteria based on Integrity:
Criterion 1 (Operational Volume Containment): (Qualitative) No probable1 single failure of the UAS or any external system supporting the operation shall lead to operation outside of the operation volume. OR (Quantitative) The probability of the failure condition “UA leaving the operational volume” shall be less than 10-3/Flight Hour (FH).

Criterion 2 (End of Flight upon exit of the operational volume): When the UA leaves the operational volume, an immediate end of the flight must be initiated through a combination of procedures/processes and/or available technical means.

Criterion 3 (Definition of the final ground risk buffer): Ground risk buffer must consider the following points below:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Probable single failures (including the projection of high energy parts such as rotors and propellers) which would lead to an operation outside of the operational volume,
    \item Meteorological conditions (e.g., maximum sustained wind),
    \item UAS latencies (e.g., latencies that affect the timely manoeuvrability of the UA),
    \item UA behaviour when activating a technical containment measure, UA performance.
\end{itemize}

Criterion 4 (Ground risk buffer containment): 
\begin{itemize}
    \item No single failure of the UAS or any external system supporting the operation shall lead to operation outside of he ground risk buffer.
    \item Software (SW) and Airborne Electronic Hardware (AEH) whose development error(s) could directly lead to operations outside of the ground risk buffer shall be developed to an industry standard or methodology recognized as adequate by the competent authority.
\end{itemize}

\section{Contingency Management}

Step 1: Propose adequate operational safety objectives;
Step 2: Identify the risks of the operation on the ground and in the air considering all of the below:
\begin{itemize}
    \item the extent to which third parties or property on the ground could be endangered by the activity;
    \item the complexity, performance and operational characteristics of the unmanned aircraft involved;
    \item the purpose of the flight, the type of UAS, the probability of collision with other aircraft and class of airspace used;
    \item the type, scale, and complexity of the UAS operation or activity, including, where relevant, the size and type of the traffic handled by the responsible organisation or person;
    \item the extent to which the persons affected by the risks involved in the UAS operation are able to assess and exercise control over those risks.
\end{itemize}

Step 3: identify a range of possible risk mitigating measures;
Step 4: determine the necessary level of robustness of the selected mitigating measures in such a way that the operation can be conducted safely.

\textbf{Risk Scenarios}
\newline
Below is a list of possible risks which might occur during operation of the aircraft:
\begin{itemize}
    \item OP-RISK01: Loss of control during flight while connection with the drone is maintained
    \item OP-RISK02: Loss of communication with the drone during the test phase
    \item OP-RISK03: Human Error
    \item OP-RISK04 Unscheduled sudden disassembly during flight
    \item OP-RISK05: Exceeding the flight envelope
    \item OP-RISK06: Exceeding the flight limit area 
    \item OP-RISK07: Air-collisions
    \item OP-RISK08: Fuel-fire
    \item OP-RISK09: Mechanical failure (e. g. LG not deploying
    \item OP-RISK10: Rapid deterioration of weather conditions during flight
\end{itemize}








\textbf{Preventive Measures for Contingency Scenarios}

For the critical scenarios that could happen during the mission, we can run preplanned scenarios to mitigate the damage. These measures can be found in the following.
\begin{itemize}
    \item For any loss of connection or control that happens for a duration longer then 10 s, a safety mechanism will be triggered automatically or manually that will turn off the engine and crash land the drone. (contingency) 
    \item Human Error (preventive)
    \item 
    \item For problems during flight that include breaking of parts or components, the drone will immediately decelerate to a safe speed (tbd) and then recovery of the vehicle will be attempted. If recovery is deemed impossible in the timespan of 10s, the first step will be applied. (contingency)
    \item Monitoring the condition of critical components (preventive) 
    \item Short service cycles on critical components (preventive)
    \item If the test area is exceeded, the drone will be ordered to immediately return to the testing location (contingency)
    \item In the case of a mechanical error onboard the drone during the test flight is detected, an analysis of the level of FUCKERY  will be assessed, and one of the 2 scenarios will be implemented: 1. The drone will be ordered to immediately return. 2. The first option will be implemented, and the drone will be lost (contingency)
    \item In case of deteriorating weather conditions, the drone will be ordered to return
    
\end{itemize}
