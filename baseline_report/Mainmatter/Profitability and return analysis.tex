\subsection{Labour costs}
This aircraft is actively developed by TU Delft students. Therefore, the human labour costs associated with the project development are not included.

\subsection{Certification costs}
The UAV will be subject to several airworthiness certification procedures. First, the operator of the aircraft has to be registered in The Netherlands under the RDW authority to get authorized for operating UAVs. The cost of this administrative action is approximately 50 €. Flying a UAV in the specific category under EASA regulation requires permit from the ILT. The fee for this procedure usually lies below 10 000 €. A cost of 5 000 € is estimated here. 

\section{Production costs}
The total production cost of the aircraft is heavily dependent on the materials and resources used. It should include direct material costs, costs of facility use and direct labour costs. The cap of 45~000 € as a maximum total cost for one unit is the biggest factor reflected in the production cost. 

\section{Direct operational cost}
Operations of the UAV include several categories of expenses, including fuel and energy costs, transport costs, insurance costs and labour costs.
\subsection{Fuel and energy costs}
In order to perform a 30-minute test flight, around 11.5 liters of SAF needs to be used. This is based on the average consumption of one VULCAN engine. Considering a SAF price of €2768 per ton --CITE EASA-- at a density of 800 $kg \cdot m^{-3}$, the price per liter of SAF lies at 2.2 € per liter. Therefore, the fuel cost per one test can be estimated at 30 €. Other energy involved in the process includes electricity used for charging the UAV batteries and powering computers and hardware needed to conduct the test. In general, these costs will be low. An extra margin of 20 € is added to the total energy cost which accounts for a sufficient margin.
