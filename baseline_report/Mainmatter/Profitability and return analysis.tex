\section{Development costs}
\subsection{Labour costs}
This aircraft is actively developed by TU Delft students. Therefore, the human labour costs associated with the project development, such as tasks associated with design, testing and manufacturing, are not included.

\subsection{Certification costs}
The UAV will be subject to several airworthiness certification procedures. First, the operator of the aircraft has to be registered in The Netherlands under the RDW authority to get authorized for operating UAVs. The cost of this administrative action is approximately 50 €. Flying a UAV in the specific category under EASA regulation requires permit from the ILT. The fee for this procedure usually lies below 10 000 €. A cost of 5 000 € is estimated here. 

\section{Production costs}
The total production cost of the aircraft is heavily dependent on the materials and resources used. The cap of 45~000 € as a maximum total cost for one unit is the biggest factor reflected in the production cost. Among the direct costs of one UAV unit, production expenses should also include costs of facility use and direct labour costs. An initial estimate of 300 man-hours at a wage of 50 € per hour is used. The UAV production process is estimated to last two working weeks (e.g. 10 working days) with a total facility use expense of 2000 € (i.e. 200 € per day).

\section{Direct operational cos}
Operations of the UAV include several categories of expenses, including fuel and energy costs, transport costs, insurance costs and labour costs.
\subsection{Fuel and energy costs}
In order to perform a 30-minute test flight, around 11.5 liters of SAF needs to be used. This is based on the average consumption of one VULCAN engine. Considering a SAF price of €2768 per ton --CITE EASA-- at a density of 800 $kg \cdot m^{-3}$, the price per liter of SAF lies at 2.2 € per liter. Therefore, the fuel cost per one test can be estimated at 30 €. Other energy involved in the process includes electricity used for charging the UAV batteries and powering computers and hardware needed to conduct the test. In general, these costs will be low. An extra margin of 20 € is added to the total energy cost which accounts for a sufficient margin.
\subsection{Transport costs}
Transport costs represent expenses associated with transporting the aircraft to the test site. This includes fuel or energy used by the transport vehicle and potential cost of leasing a vehicle or hiring an external driver. Assuming that the aircraft is mostly going to be used for testing within The Netherlands, these costs can be estimated at 250 € per test.
\subsection{Insurance costs}
Since the aircraft will have a maximum take-off mass larger than 20 kg, legislation requires arranging a third-party insurance. The annual price of insurance for a similar-sized UAV usually ranges from lower to higher hundreds of €. Therefore, an estimate of an annual price of 500 € has been used.
